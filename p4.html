Saudi Arabia

The Makkah Royal Clock Tower is the 3rd tallest building in the world. The address is Makkah Haram, Mecca, Saudi Arabia. It was design by Dar Al-Hadassah Shair and Partners; SL Rasch and it was a 10 year project that started in 2002 and was completed in 2012. It was developed as a part of the King Abdul-Aziz Endowment Project and provides a place for the journey of Muslims during their Hajj period.

Accommodating many worshippers, the clock tower stands in the center of a high-rise complex called Abraj Al-Bait. The structure stands at 1,972 ft. and the other towers surrounding the center varies in heights and services to other visitors. The clock also holds the record for the largest and highest in the world.

In front of the tower is the Kaaba, the stone structured shaped like a box that has been demolished and rebuilt several times in history. Tradition says that the Kaaba was built by Adam and rebuilt by Abraham and descendants of Noah; known as the House of God. Muslim face towards it to pray during their hajj. Inside the box is a black stone that many believe that it originated from a meteor and it is worshipped as a heavenly relic.
